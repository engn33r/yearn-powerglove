# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Yearn Powerglove is for aesthetically sensitive power DeFi analysts and prosumers who already understand vaults, chains, APY, TVL, and strategy behavior. They are comparing opportunities, inspecting vault health, reading allocation changes, and validating whether a vault deserves attention or capital.

These users value density, but not clutter. They want fast scanning, trustworthy numbers, crisp hierarchy, and enough context to understand why a vault is interesting without being slowed down by generic education or decorative crypto theatrics.

## Product Purpose

Yearn Powerglove is a vault explorer for Yearn vaults. It helps users compare vaults across chains, inspect individual vault performance, understand strategy allocation, follow management events, and spot vaults that need deeper research.

Success means the interface feels expert, calm, and legible under repeated use. A user should be able to move from list scanning to vault-level analysis without losing orientation, second-guessing data hierarchy, or fighting ornamental UI.

## Brand Personality

Calm, sharp, editorial.

The product should feel like a well-edited analytical surface: deliberate, composed, and intelligent. It can have a point of view, but it should never perform expertise through noise. The voice should be concise and exact, with labels and supporting copy that respect domain-literate users.

## Anti-references

Avoid boring SaaS dashboard tropes: interchangeable cards, soft corporate sameness, decorative gradients, generic metrics, and visual hierarchy that looks assembled from defaults.

Avoid generic overstuffed AI dashboard patterns: crowded panels, arbitrary summaries, excessive badges, vague assistant-like phrasing, and surfaces that look impressive before they become useful.

Avoid gamified slop: achievement language, reward aesthetics, toy-like visuals, casino energy, neon crypto terminal clichés, and playful interactions that make serious analysis feel unserious.

## Design Principles

Make density feel edited. Preserve information richness, but use spacing, typography, grouping, and progressive disclosure so the page reads cleanly instead of feeling packed.

Lead with analytical trust. Numbers, chart states, filters, errors, and overrides should make their source and meaning clear enough that power users can rely on them.

Prefer editorial hierarchy over dashboard ornament. Use scale, rhythm, alignment, and selective emphasis to create interest instead of decorative cards, gradients, or gratuitous icons.

Respect domain fluency. Do not over-explain common DeFi concepts, but do surface the right context when a data point is derived, unavailable, overridden, or risky.

Keep interaction quiet and fast. Search, filtering, sorting, chart inspection, and vault navigation should feel immediate, predictable, and keyboard-friendly where practical.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Use a basic accessibility bar suitable for production product UI: keyboard-accessible controls, visible focus states, semantic structure, readable contrast, and reduced-motion support for nonessential animation.

Charts and status indicators should not rely on color alone. Use labels, position, shape, or text where needed so color-blind users can still interpret important states.
